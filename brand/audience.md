## Primary ICPs (Director-Level Decision Makers)

### 1. Enterprise Pharma

**Titles:** Director Patient Support, VP Patient Services, Executive Director Patient Support Solutions, Senior Director Patient Services, Head of Patient Services

**Companies:** Gilead, Lundbeck, Ipsen, Teva, Biogen, Covis, Sobi, Mainstay Medical

**Responsibilities:**
- Strategy and operations of Patient Support Programs (PSPs)
- Hub and ecosystem strategy across channel partners
- Vendor selection and management
- Commercial launch excellence
- Collaboration with brand, marketing, legal, managed markets, payer strategy

**Pain points:**
- **3–8% utilization:** Pharma invests $22B+ annually in PSPs; only 3–8% of eligible patients use them (Phreesia, GoodRx, Zelthy data)
- **The "missing middle":** Operational barriers (prior auth, copay, specialty pharmacy) are solved. Psychological barriers (fear, overwhelm, denial) are not. Patients enroll but don't start therapy
- **ROI pressure:** Need to justify spend. "Engagement" metrics that don't tie to commercial results (starts, adherence, retention) don't land
- **Compliance risk:** Regulatory scrutiny on PSPs. Every vendor adds compliance burden
- **Awareness gap:** 59% of patients have little/no knowledge of PSPs. Programs exist but patients don't find them

**Wants / needs:**
- Programs that move the needle on starts and adherence—not just "engagement"
- Behavioral intelligence that meets patients where decisions actually happen (emotional, not just logistical)
- Measurable outcomes: % increase in starts, adherence, days on therapy
- Compliance-first vendors (HIPAA, SOC 2, audit trails)
- Integration with existing hub/ecosystem (not another silo)
- Proof from similar brands (rare disease, specialty, launch)

**Language they use:** "Patient journey," "adherence," "persistence," "commercial success," "launch excellence," "patient access," "hub services," "utilization," "ROI"

### 2. Enterprise Med-Tech

**Titles:** Director Patient Engagement, Principal Patient Marketing Specialist, Director Product Marketing and Patient Advocacy

**Companies:** Medtronic, Varian/Siemens Healthineers, Boston Scientific, Abiomed, Insulet, Mainstay Medical

**Responsibilities:**
- Patient experience and advocacy strategy
- Patient engagement across product lifecycle
- Multi-channel patient marketing
- Collaboration with HCPs, health systems, patient advocacy groups

**Pain points:**
- Device adoption and training—patients and providers need support to use complex devices
- Patient engagement lags pharma; med-tech has been slower to invest
- Omnichannel expectations—customers want tailored, on-demand experiences
- Differentiation in crowded device categories

**Wants / needs:**
- Patient programs that improve device adoption and outcomes
- Peer support for patients navigating new devices (e.g., neuromodulation, implants)
- Compliance with medical device regulations
- Evidence that programs drive commercial results

**Language they use:** "Patient engagement," "patient advocacy," "lifecycle management," "adoption," "outcomes," "omnichannel"

### 3. Clinical Trial Divisions (Pharma + CRO)

**Titles:** Director Clinical Operations, Head of Recruitment and Retention, VP Clinical Development

**Companies:** Pharma clinical divisions, CROs (Syneos, IQVIA, WCG, etc.)

**Pain points:**
- **85% of trials fail to meet enrollment targets** (WCG 2024)
- **80% of study delays** linked to recruitment
- 15–40% dropout rates after enrollment
- $600K–$8M cost per day of delay to market
- Sites lack staff for recruitment; participants need support to enroll and stay

**Wants / needs:**
- Recruitment solutions that identify and enroll the right participants
- Retention programs that reduce dropout
- Participant support (financial, travel, emotional) that removes barriers
- Peer mentorship for trial participants (e.g., "someone who's been through this trial")
- Data and analytics on recruitment/retention performance

**Language they use:** "Enrollment," "retention," "recruitment," "participant experience," "site burden," "trial timeline"

## Decision-Making Triggers

| Trigger | What moves them |
|---------|-----------------|
| **ROI** | Specific numbers: 68% more starts, 71% decision impact, 29% better adherence, 133.5 more days on therapy |
| **Differentiation** | "Peer-to-peer beats traditional marketing." Proof that patient trust has shifted |
| **Compliance** | HIPAA, SOC 2, ISO 27001. "We can scale without scaling risk" |
| **The missing middle** | "We solve the psychological barriers—fear, doubt, lack of relatable support—that hubs don't touch" |
| **Speed to value** | "Launches in under four months." "Real-time connections when patients are ready" |
| **Social proof** | Named clients (Sobi, Mainstay). Testimonials from Directors/VPs |
| **Champion resonance** | Personal experience with patient support; frustration with current vendor; new drug launch |
